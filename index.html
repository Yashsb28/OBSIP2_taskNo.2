M. S. Dhoni

The Captain Cool

Biography

Mahendra Singh Dhoni (born July 7, 1981) is a former Indian international cricketer who captained the Indian national team from 2007 to 2017. He is considered to be one of the greatest captains in the history of Indian cricket, leading India to victory in the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cups, the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy.

Dhoni is also known for his excellent wicket-keeping skills, having affected the most number of stumpings in international cricket. He has won numerous awards for his contributions to Indian cricket, including the ICC ODI Player of the Year award in 2008 and 2009 and the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award in 2007. Dhoni retired from international cricket in August 2020, leaving behind a legacy that will be remembered for years to come.
